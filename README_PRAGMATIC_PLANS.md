## ğŸ“ QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Q: Why not build the Rust/SWC compiler?

**A:** Babel is fast enough (~1ms/file). Rust rewrite would take months, introduce risks, and delay v2.0. We can revisit in v3.0 if compilation speed becomes a proven bottleneck.

**See:** [RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md - Rust/SWC Compiler](./RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md#1-rustsWC-compiler-rewrite)

### Q: Why not integrate Million.js?

**A:** Million.js is experimental and not proven at scale. Fine-grained reactivity already avoids most re-renders. Integration would be complex and risky with limited benefit.

**See:** [RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md - Million.js](./RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md#2-millionjs-block-dom-integration)

### Q: Why defer Type-safe CSS to v2.1?

**A:** Cool feature, but not critical for v2.0. Vanilla Extract and other solutions already exist. Would take weeks to implement properly. Better to ship core v2.0 first.

**See:** [RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md - Type-safe CSS](./RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md#10-type-safe-css)

### Q: What if we fall behind schedule?

**A:** Cut scope. Mark PPR/Islands as experimental. Extend timeline. Focus on MVP. See risk mitigation strategies.

**See:** [PRAGMATIC_V2_PLAN.md - Risk Mitigations](./PRAGMATIC_V2_PLAN.md#8-risk-mitigations)

### Q: How do we say no to feature requests?

**A:** Use the decision matrix and template responses. Defer to v2.1 if not critical. Focus on v2.0 ship blockers.

**See:** [RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md - Saying No](./RUTHLESS_CUT_LIST.md#saying-no-template-responses)
